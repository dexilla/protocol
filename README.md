## Problem Statement:
The existing landscape of decentralized exchanges (DEX) presents several challenges and limitations that hinder the optimal trading experience for users. These challenges include liquidity constraints, high gas fees, scalability issues, and a lack of user control over funds.

Traditional DEX platforms often struggle with liquidity, resulting in fragmented and limited pools of available assets for trading. This lack of liquidity restricts the trading opportunities for users, making it difficult to execute large trades efficiently and impacting market efficiency.

High gas fees on Ethereum-based DEX platforms pose a significant barrier to entry and profitability. Users are burdened by substantial transaction costs, reducing the overall profitability of trades and deterring active participation.

Scalability issues further exacerbate the challenges faced by traders, leading to slow transaction speeds and network congestion during peak trading periods. This not only causes delays but also frustrates users who expect fast and seamless trade execution.

Moreover, the lack of user control over funds is a prevalent concern. Users often have to deposit their assets into smart contracts controlled by the DEX platform, introducing counterparty risk and compromising the principles of decentralization and self-custody.

Addressing these challenges is crucial to improving the trading experience and driving the adoption of decentralized exchanges. Dexilla aims to overcome these limitations by providing enhanced liquidity, lower gas fees, improved scalability, and empowering users with full control over their assets.
